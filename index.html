Tribute to Tony Elumelu

The Grit Man with a Million Heart

Here's a timeline for Tony Elumelu's Life and impact Across Africa:

  • 1963 - Born in Jos, Plateau State, Nigeria.
  • 1989 - At 26, he was appointed branch manager All States Bank port harcout; his branch was credited for making more money than 34 other branches of the bank put together, withing a short period of resumption.
  • 1993 - Got married to Awele Vivien, a medical doctor.
  • 1997 - He became the youngest CEO of a commercial bank(STB), in the Nigerian book of records.
  • 2003 - The federal govt of Nigeria granted TOE the title of MFR.
  • 2006 - He was voted best African business leader of the year by a magazine.
  • 2008 - He won the award for Africa Banker of the year.
  • 2009 - He was appointed by the president to serve on the presidential committee on the Global financial crisis.
  • 2010 - He retired from UBA, Founded Heir Holdings, Tony Elumelu Foundation.
  • 2011 - He was appointed chairman Transcorp(Transnational Corporation of Nigeria). He also chaired a ministerial committee to establish world class hospitals and diagnostic centres, also engineered the creation of 3million jobs in one year.
  • 2012 - He was awarded the prestigious National Honour of Commander of the Order of the Niger (CON) for his service in promoting private enterprise. Also, He was recognised as one of "Africa's 20 Most Powerful People by Forbes magazine. In addition, New African magazine featured him in their list of the "100 Most Influential Africans in Business". He was awarded an honorary doctorate of science degree from Benue State University and an honorary doctorate of business administration from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.
  • 2013 - He received the Leadership Award in Business and Philanthropy from the Africa-America Institute (AAI) Awards. He was also named African Business Icon at the African Business Awards.
  • 2014 Launched a $100 million Entrepreneurship program for 10,000 entrepreneurs accross 54 African countries over a 10 year period to empower the next generation of african entrepreneurs.
  • 2015 - ESI-Africa, frequently described as "Africa's power journal", named him in its list as "ESI Most Influential Figures in African Power"
  • 2016 - He was awarded a Lifetime Achievement Award at the 5th edition of the annual Economic Forum of the Ivorian National Council of Employers, CGECI Academy, (CGECI) in Abidjan.
